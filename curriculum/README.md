# Intentional Fathers Small Group — Facilitator Curriculum Guide

Based on *The Intentional Father* by Jon Tyson
Man of War Church · Lexington, KY
September 14 – October 26, 2026

---

## How to Use This Guide

This curriculum maps Jon Tyson's book to a 7-week small group format. Each week covers 2–3 chapters. The group is designed for 10 dads max.

**Format for each session (60–90 minutes):**
1. Opening prayer (2 min)
2. Check-in: "What step did you commit to last week? How did it go?" (10–15 min)
3. Teach/Discussion: Walk through the week's content using the questions below (30–40 min)
4. Book discussion: Key passages, pushback, application (15–20 min)
5. Commitment: Each man states one concrete step for the coming week (10 min)
6. Prayer partners: Pair up, pray for each other (5 min)

**Ground rules (establish Week 1):**
- What's said here stays here
- No preaching at each other — share your own struggles
- No fixing — listen first, advise only when asked
- Come prepared (reading done, honest with yourself)

## Week 1: The Need for Fathers
**Reading:** Chapters 1–2 (Part 1: Introduction)
**Key Scripture:** Malachi 4:6; Ephesians 6:4

### Key Content
- The crisis of fatherhood today — why young men are struggling to become men
- Five kinds of fathers (Tyson's framework):
  1. **Absent Father** — physically or emotionally gone
  2. **Ignorant Father** — means well but doesn't know what to do
  3. **Passive Father** — present but disengaged
  4. **Reactive Father** — responds to problems but doesn't initiate
  5. **Intentional Father** — proactive, purposeful, present
- The principle: A father's presence, emotional health, and involvement give his children a tremendous advantage
- Cultural data: What the research says about father involvement and child outcomes

### Discussion Questions
1. "Which kind of father are you right now? Be honest — not which one you want to be."
2. "Which kind of father did you grow up with? How has that shaped how you parent?"
3. "What's one area where you know you've been coasting?"
4. Tyson opens the book with his own anxiety upon learning he was having a son. When have you felt that kind of fear about fatherhood?

### For the Book
- Ask: "Did any of the five father types surprise you or hit close to home?"
- Key quote: Tyson's argument that self-initiation is killing our young men — without mentors, boys walk alone into confusion

### Sample Commitment Prompts
- "This week, I will [specific action] with my son/children."
- Examples: Initiate a 1-on-1 conversation. Put the phone down during dinner. Pray specifically for one child by name.

## Week 2: A Preview of the Possible / Honoring Your Father
**Reading:** Chapters 3–4 (Part 2: Preparation)
**Key Scripture:** Joshua 24:15; Exodus 20:12

### Key Content
- **Part 2: Preparation** — before you can lead your son, you must prepare yourself
- Chapter 3 — A Preview of the Possible:
  - What intentional fatherhood actually looks like in practice
  - The father as the architect of home culture
  - Moving from passive to proactive fathering
- Chapter 4 — Honoring Your Father:
  - Even broken fathers deserve honor (the commandment doesn't come with an exception clause)
  - Breaking free from bitterness toward your own father
  - Choosing to give your sons what you didn't receive yourself

### Discussion Questions
1. "What does 'intentional fatherhood' look like in your home this week — concretely?"
2. "If you wrote a one-paragraph vision for your household, what would it say?"
3. "Have you made peace with your own father — even if he failed you? Where are you in that process?"
4. "What are you repeating from your father that you don't want to repeat?"

### For the Book
- Chapter 4 is often the hardest. Some dads will need to sit with this for weeks. Don't rush it.
- Pushback to expect: "You don't know what my father did to me." — Acknowledge the pain. The call to honor is not the same as approval.

### Sample Commitment Prompts
- "I will write down 3 values I want to define my home by."
- "I will have a conversation with someone about my father — a mentor, counselor, or trusted friend."

## Week 3: Ceremony, Home & Values
**Reading:** Chapters 5–7 (Part 3: Initiation)
**Key Scripture:** Deuteronomy 6:4–9; Proverbs 22:6

### Key Content
- **Part 3: Initiation** — marking the transition, establishing identity
- Chapter 5 — Ceremony:
  - The power of "marking moments" — rites of passage matter
  - Creating intentional ceremonies that mark milestones in your son's life
  - Liminal space: the transitional phase between boyhood and manhood
- Chapter 6 — Home:
  - Your home is the primary discipleship environment
  - Establishing rituals, rhythms, and routines that shape identity
  - The father as the culture-setter of the home
- Chapter 7 – Values:
  - Defining your family's non-negotiable values
  - Teaching values by modeling, not just lecturing
  - The difference between rules and values

### Discussion Questions
1. "What 'marking moments' do your kids remember? What moments did you intentionally create?"
2. "What are the non-negotiable values of your household? Can your kids articulate them?"
3. "If your home had a 'culture,' how would you describe it right now?"
4. "What's one ceremony or tradition you could start this month?"

### For the Book
- Tyson's concept of "liminal space" is key — the uncomfortable in-between where transformation happens. Fathers need to intentionally guide sons through these seasons.

### Sample Commitment Prompts
- "I will plan and execute one intentional 'marking moment' with my child this week."
- "I will define 5 family values and share them with my wife/kids."

## Week 4: The Five Shifts
**Reading:** Chapter 8 (Part 4: Formation)
**Key Scripture:** 2 Timothy 2:2; Philippians 4:8–9

### Key Content
- **Part 4: Formation** — the long work of shaping character
- The five shifts every father must make:
  1. **From unhealthy to healthy identity** — knowing who you are in Christ first
  2. **From comfort to courage** — choosing the hard right over the easy wrong
  3. **From isolated to connected** — you cannot raise sons alone
  4. **From reactive to proactive** — initiating rather than responding
  5. **From self-focused to others-focused** — your family, your church, your community
- Practical application: How to model each shift for your sons

### Discussion Questions
1. "Which of the five shifts is the hardest for you right now? Why?"
2. "Where have you seen yourself move from reactive to proactive this month?"
3. "Who are the men holding you accountable? If the answer is 'no one,' what does that tell you?"
4. "What's one 'comfortable' thing you need to step away from to be a better father?"

### For the Book
- This is the hinge week. The first 3 weeks built the vision. Now the real work begins.

### Sample Commitment Prompts
- "I will initiate one conversation this week that I've been avoiding."
- "I will ask another man in this group to hold me accountable on one specific thing."

## Week 5: Preparing for Moments / Being Good at Being a Man
**Reading:** Chapters 9–10 (Part 4: Formation continued)
**Key Scripture:** Matthew 7:11; Psalm 103:13; 1 Corinthians 16:13

### Key Content
- Chapter 9 — The Power of Preparing for Moments:
  - Don't let the defining moments of your son's life happen by accident
  - Anticipating key conversations (sex, faith, identity, failure, calling)
  - Preparing your heart before you prepare your words
- Chapter 10 — Being Good at Being a Man:
  - What biblical masculinity actually looks like (vs. cultural confusion)
  - Strength, courage, tenderness, leadership — all biblical
  - Teaching your son that "being a man" = knowing your identity and living it with conviction
  - Standing firm against negative cultural trends

### Discussion Questions
1. "Which conversation with your son are you most afraid of having? What would it look like to prepare for it?"
2. "How do you define 'being a man' biblically? How is that different from how culture defines it?"
3. "Where have you seen your son struggle with identity or confusion? How have you responded?"
4. "What's the most important uninitiated conversation you need to have with your son in the next 6 months?"

### For the Book
- This is where the group gets real. Expect vulnerability. Don't let it become therapy — keep pointing back to Scripture and forward to action.

### Sample Commitment Prompts
- "I will initiate a key conversation with my son this week that I've been putting off."
- "I will pray specifically for my son/son's future identity and calling."

## Week 6: Roles, Identity & the Arc of Life
**Reading:** Chapters 11–13 (Part 4: Formation continued)
**Key Scripture:** James 1:2–4; Romans 8:28–29; Ecclesiastes 3:1–8

### Key Content
- Chapter 11 — Roles to Master:
  - The roles a father plays: provider, protector, teacher, priest, friend
  - Recognizing which roles come naturally and which require growth
  - Mastering the roles your family needs most right now
- Chapter 12 — Self-Discovery:
  - Helping your son discover who God made him to be
  - Identifying giftedness, passions, and calling
  - The father as a mirror: reflecting truth about who your child is
- Chapter 13 — The Arc of Life:
  - Understanding the seasons of fatherhood
  - Adapting your approach as your children grow (infancy → childhood → adolescence → young adulthood)
  - What your son needs from you NOW — and what he'll need in 5 years

### Discussion Questions
1. "Which roles come naturally to you? Which ones feel like a stretch?"
2. "Where are you in the 'arc of life' with your oldest son? Has your approach adapted?"
3. "Have you helped your son discover his unique identity and gifting — or have you projected yours onto him?"
4. "What does your son need from you today that's different from what he needed a year ago?"

### For the Book
- This is where the fruit starts showing. Men who've been doing the work for 5 weeks are starting to see changes.

### Sample Commitment Prompts
- "I will take my son on a 1-on-1 outing this week and ask him three questions about who he thinks he is."
- "I will identify the role I'm weakest in and ask one man in this group how he handles it."

## Week 7: Recognition & Legacy
**Reading:** Chapters 14–16 (Part 5: Recognition)
**Key Scripture:** Joshua 24:15; 2 Timothy 2:2; Psalm 78:1–7

### Key Content
- **Part 5: Recognition** — celebrating faithfulness and planning the next season
- Chapter 14 — The Gap Year:
  - What happens after intentional season — don't drift
  - The gap year: a season of exploration and growth for your son
  - How to provide structure and freedom simultaneously
- Chapter 15 — Ceremony of Welcome and Blessing:
  - The power of a father's spoken blessing over his son
  - Planning your own "welcome ceremony" when the time comes
  - Words have power — speak life over your children
- Chapter 16 — The Intentional Father:
  - The journey continues — this isn't a program, it's a way of life
  - Building a plan for the next season of your family
  - Committing to the men at your side: the brotherhood that remains

### Discussion Questions
1. "What's the one thing from these 7 weeks that changed how you lead your home?"
2. "Have you ever spoken a blessing over your son? What would that look like?"
3. "What's your biggest takeaway — and what are you committed to keeping going?"
4. "Who in this group are you going to stay connected with? How?"

### Closing the Group
- Go around the room: each man shares one commitment for the next 30 days
- Prayer: Pair up, pray over each other's families
- Plan the next gathering (even if informal — a monthly lunch, a text thread, etc.)

### For the Book
- End with Joshua 24:15: "As for me and my house, we will serve the Lord."
- Challenge: "The book ends here. But your story as an intentional father doesn't."

### Sample Commitment Prompts
- "I will speak a specific blessing over my son this week."
- "I will set up a monthly check-in with at least one man from this group."
- "I will write a one-page family vision statement."

## Facilitator Notes

### Common Pushbacks (and how to handle them)

**"I didn't have a father, so I don't know what to do."**
→ That's exactly why you're here. You're breaking the cycle. Tyson addresses this directly in Chapter 4.

**"My kids are already grown. Is this too late?"**
→ No. You can still speak blessing, still be intentional, still lead. The "arc of life" content in Chapter 13 addresses this.

**"My wife does most of the parenting."**
→ Then why do you think Tyson wrote this book for fathers? Your role is not optional.

**"I don't have time to read."**
→ You have time for what matters. If your family matters, the reading matters. Be gentle but honest.

**"This is too intense / emotional / weird."**
→ Good. Comfort isn't the goal formation is. Tyson's whole premise: self-initiation is killing young men. This is the antidote.

### Group Dynamics to Watch For

- **The Fixer:** Man who gives advice constantly. Redirect: "Let's hear from someone who hasn't spoken yet."
- **The Silent Guy:** Man who never shares. Draw out privately, not publicly. Trust takes time.
- **The Over-sharer:** Man who dominates with his story. Gently redirect to the question at hand.
- **The Critic:** Man who pushes back on everything. Acknowledge his point, move on, follow up privately.

### Supplies Needed Each Week

- Bibles (encourage guys to bring their own)
- Printed discussion questions (optional — most guys will follow conversationally)
- Name tags (Week 1–2 only)
- Coffee/snacks (builds community, keeps guys from leaving right away)
